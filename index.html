1. Executive Summary

Gift Registry is a new solution within Wishlist Plus that enables shoppers to create a registry for a life event (wedding, baby shower, housewarming, birthday), add products they want, and share the registry with friends and family who can purchase items on their behalf — with full address privacy.

The feature introduces a fundamentally different intent signal than wishlists. A wishlist is a "maybe." A registry is a "mission" — it has a deadline, an audience, and a built-in conversion engine. Every registry created turns one shopper (the Creator) into a distribution channel that brings 5–10 new customers (the Givers) to the merchant at zero acquisition cost. This is the core strategic thesis: a registry is a customer acquisition engine disguised as a gifting feature.

The MVP takes a privacy-first approach. Address Masking removes the primary barrier to sharing (Creators don't have to reveal their home address to gift-senders). Live Tracking eliminates duplicate purchases. A simplified Draft Order checkout removes friction for Givers. The result is a registry experience where the Creator feels safe sharing widely, Givers can buy confidently, and the merchant captures new customer identities and revenue they would otherwise lose.

Core Hypothesis

A privacy-first registry turns every Creator into an acquisition channel. One Creator generates 5–10 Giver visits. Givers convert at higher rates and higher AOV than standard traffic because they are on a mission to buy a gift the Creator actually wants. The merchant captures Giver email addresses as new customer identities at zero acquisition cost.

MVP Target: End of Q1 2026. Enterprise + Premium plan merchants. Hybrid launch: 5–10 pilot merchants, then broader rollout within 30 days.

2. Strategic Context & Outcome

2.1 Problem Statement

Missing a registry feature results in three measurable losses for merchants:

Lost opportunity cost. Merchants in gifting-adjacent categories (Home & Garden, Wedding, Toys & Hobbies, Gifts & Special Events, Books & Literature) have no native mechanism to facilitate coordinated gifting. Shoppers who want to create a registry for a life event leave the merchant's store and use a generic registry platform (Amazon, Zola, MyRegistry), taking the revenue, the customer data, and the Giver relationships with them.

Return tax of 25–30% on luxury gifts. Without live tracking of what has already been purchased, Givers frequently buy duplicate items or products the Creator doesn't actually want. The industry return rate for gifts — especially in luxury and home categories — runs 25–30%. Each return is an operational cost for the merchant and a failed experience for the Giver.

Identity leak. When Givers purchase through a third-party registry platform, the merchant loses the Giver's identity entirely. These are high-intent, new-to-brand customers who are ready to spend — and the merchant never captures their email, never retargets them, and never converts them into repeat buyers. The Giver data is lost.

Additionally, from the shopper's perspective:

The "Awkwardness" Factor. Creators often feel uncomfortable directly asking for gifts or sharing their personal shipping addresses due to privacy concerns.

The Duplicate/Return Loop. Without live tracking, Givers frequently buy duplicate items or products the Creator doesn't want, leading to high return rates and a negative experience for both parties.

Missed Acquisition. Merchants lack a structured mechanism to identify and convert Givers, who represent a significant pool of high-intent, new-to-brand customers.

Omni-channel Blind Spots. Store associates often lack context on a shopper's registry intent during in-person visits, resulting in missed service opportunities.

2.2 Strategic Alignment

Gift Registry directly supports Swym's overarching vision of capturing and activating shopper intent at scale. The registry is the highest-intent list type on the platform — it has a deadline, a social distribution mechanism, and a built-in conversion incentive. Where a wishlist captures individual product interest, a registry captures life-event intent and extends it to a network of Givers.

The feature spans all three product areas:

  • Swym for Marketing: Event-driven triggers (milestone reminders, low stock alerts, price drop notifications) and Giver retargeting.
  • Swym for Sales: Clienteling via Shopify POS — associates can look up a customer's registry during in-store visits.
  • Core Platform: Gift Registry is the validation vehicle for the List Platform architecture. It is the first complex solution ported to the List API, proving the platform approach before subsequent solutions (B2B Shopping List, Curated Lists) are built.

2.3 Target Outcome

The specific outcome is to make the merchant's store the default destination for life-event gifting, demonstrated by:

  • # of registries created
  • Registry-attributed revenue (direct contribution to SAV).
  • Giver identities captured (new customer emails acquired at zero cost).
  • Viral coefficient (unique Giver visitors per registry created).

From the merchant side, the desired outcome is that Gift Registry becomes a standard offering for merchants in gifting-adjacent categories, with measurable adoption and repeat usage.

2.4 Target Audience

Merchants: Enterprise + Premium plan merchants on Wishlist Plus. Initial seeding in five high-affinity verticals: Home & Garden, Wedding, Toys & Hobbies, Gifts & Special Events, Books & Literature. 815 existing Wishlist Plus merchants fall within these categories and represent the immediate addressable market.

Shoppers (Creators): Anyone celebrating a life event who shops at a merchant with Gift Registry enabled. The primary use cases are wedding registries, baby shower registries, housewarming registries, and birthday wish lists.

Shoppers (Givers): Friends and family of the Creator who receive the registry link. These are the acquisition targets — many will be new to the merchant's brand.

2.5 Assumptions

  • A Wishlist is a "maybe"; a Registry is a "mission."
  • One Creator brings in 5–10 Givers at zero acquisition cost.
  • Address privacy is the primary barrier to broad registry sharing. Removing it via Address Masking will measurably increase sharing rates.
  • 800+ existing Wishlist Plus merchants are from categories ideal for Gift Registry. If we include Starter and Pro merchants, and FREE merchants on higher Shopify plans, this number is much higher.
  • Givers convert at higher rates and higher AOV than standard site traffic because they have purchase intent and a specific product target.

3. Core Personas and Goals

3.1 Creator

The shopper who creates the registry.

Goals: Avoid the awkwardness of directly asking for gifts. Provide a seamless way for friends and family to buy gifts they actually want. Maintain privacy (especially their shipping address). Track progress toward completion.

Key needs:

  • Simple, guided registry creation.[a][b]
  • Includes populating the registry: Curated recommendations that the brand provides, AI-assistance for adding recommended items.
  • Full control over privacy settings (address masking, purchase anonymization).[c][d][e]
  • Real-time visibility into registry activity (views, purchases, completion progress).[f][g][h]
  • Easy sharing via link copy, email, or SMS.
  • Confidence that duplicates won't happen.

3.2 Giver

The friend or family member who purchases a gift from the registry.

Goals: Buy something the Creator actually wants. Avoid duplicates. Complete the purchase with minimal friction. Optionally hand-deliver the gift in person.[i]

Key needs:

  • Clear view of what's available, what's been purchased, and what's most wanted.
  • Frictionless checkout — no account creation, no address entry (unless shipping to self[j]).[k]
  • Confirmation that their purchase was successful and the Creator has been notified.[l]

3.3 Sales Associate

The store associate assisting customers in-store or online.

Goals: Have context on a customer's registry intent during service interactions. Facilitate in-store registry purchases.

Key needs:

  • Ability to look up a customer's registry by name or email.
  • Read-only view of registry items and fulfillment status.
  • Context during in-store visits to provide personalized service.

3.4 Merchant

The brand/store that enables Gift Registry.

Goals: Facilitate gifting to increase revenue. Reduce return rates. Identify and capture new customers (Givers). Increase average order value.

Key needs:

  • Simple enablement (under 5 minutes from toggle to live).[m]
  • Reporting on registry-attributed revenue, Giver acquisition, and funnel performance.
  • Integration with existing marketing automation (Klaviyo, Shopify Flow) for registry-triggered campaigns.[n]

4. Feature Overview

4.1 Conceptual Model

A Gift Registry is a new list type (registry) within the existing Swym List architecture. It follows the same API patterns and data model as other list types (Wishlist, Saved for Later, Persistent Cart) with additional metadata for event details, privacy settings, and Giver tracking. The key conceptual shift from a Wishlist is that a registry is a shared, mission-driven list with a deadline and an audience, which fundamentally changes the interaction patterns (sharing is primary, not secondary) and the business model (acquisition of Givers, not just retention of the Creator).

4.2 Core Feature Pillars

Address Masking. Full privacy for the Creator. The Creator's shipping address is stored by Swym and injected server-side into the Shopify order. The Giver never sees the Creator's address during checkout. The Creator configures during setup whether Givers can optionally ship to their own address (for hand-delivery) or must ship to the Creator's masked address.

Live Tracking. Real-time purchase tracking visible to both Creator and Giver. When a Giver purchases an item, the registry updates immediately to show the item as purchased (or partially purchased for multi-quantity items). This eliminates duplicate purchases — the single most common registry failure.

Draft Order Checkout. Simplified checkout restricted to registry items. Swym creates a Shopify Draft Order via the Admin API with the Creator's address pre-filled. The Giver provides only their email address and payment information. No account creation, no address entry (unless they choose to ship to their own address for hand-delivery).

Clienteling. Read-only registry visibility for store associates via Shopify POS. Associates can look up a customer's registry during in-store visits and provide informed, contextual service.

Marketing Triggers. Automated notifications for Creators (view alerts, purchase confirmations, milestone reminders, low stock and price drop alerts) and integration with merchant marketing automation for Giver retargeting.

4.3 Two-Tiered Architecture

Consistent with the platform approach:

  • Tier 1 — Platform APIs: Core registry capability via the List API. Registry creation, item management, sharing, purchase-marking, Giver checkout, and event emission all operate through the List API with registry-specific configuration.

  • Tier 2 — Solution UX: The Creator setup flow, registry management page, Giver-facing registry page, merchant admin configuration, and POS integration — all built on top of the APIs.

5. User Journeys

5.1 Creator Journey

5.1.1 Registry Creation (Guided Setup)

The Creator initiates registry creation from the merchant's storefront. The flow is a guided, step-by-step setup:

Step 1 — Occasion details. Creator selects the occasion type (wedding, baby shower, housewarming, birthday, graduation, holiday, other) and enters the event name, event date, and registrant name(s). For co-registrant occasions (e.g., wedding), both names are captured.

Step 2 — Shipping address. Creator provides the shipping address where gifts should be delivered. The system confirms that this address will be kept private and never shown to Givers.

Step 3 — Privacy configuration. Creator configures:

  • Address Masking: on by default. Creator is informed that Givers will not see their address.
  • Allow Giver self-shipping: toggle (default: off). When enabled, Givers see an option during checkout to ship to their own address instead of the Creator's. When disabled, all gifts ship to the Creator's masked address.
  • Purchase anonymization: on by default. When enabled, the Creator does not see which specific Giver purchased which item until they choose to reveal (preserves the surprise element). The Creator sees only that an item has been purchased and the updated fulfillment count.

Step 4 — Confirmation and sharing. Creator is shown a preview of their registry page (empty, with event details displayed). A shareable link is generated. The Creator can copy the link immediately or proceed to add items first.

Target: Under 3 minutes from start to shareable registry.

5.1.2 Adding Items

After setup, the Creator browses the merchant's store and adds items to their registry from the Product Detail Page (PDP). The "Add to Registry" interaction is detailed in Section 6.3.

For each item, the Creator can:

  • Set a desired quantity (default: 1).
  • Add a note (e.g., "prefer the blue variant").
  • Set a priority label (must-have, nice-to-have, dream item).

Items added to the registry are visible in the Creator's registry management view within My Lists.

5.1.3 Sharing

The Creator shares the registry via:

  • Link copy: Primary sharing mechanism. The Creator copies the registry URL and distributes it however they prefer (text message, social media, email, printed invitation).
  • Email share (future scope): Creator can invite Givers via email address or phone number directly from the registry management page. This unlocks Giver targeting via email/SMS. Not in MVP.

5.1.4 Ongoing Management

The Creator's management view within My Lists shows:

  • All registry items with fulfillment status (purchased, partially purchased, unpurchased).
  • Registry progress (e.g., "7 of 12 items purchased").
  • Event countdown (days until event date).
  • Sharing controls (link copy, privacy settings).
  • Item management (add, remove, reorder, edit quantity/notes/priority).

5.1.5 Post-Event

After the event date, the registry remains active until the expiry date (default: event date + 90 days, merchant-configurable). The Creator can:

  • Mark the registry as completed at any time.
  • Archive the registry.
  • View the final fulfillment report.
  • Send thank-you notes to Givers (future scope).

5.2 Giver Journey

5.2.1 Accessing the Registry

The Giver receives the registry link from the Creator (via text, email, social media, or any other channel). Clicking the link opens the public-facing registry page on the merchant's storefront. No account or login is required.

5.2.2 Browsing the Registry

The Giver sees:

  • Event details (occasion name, event date, registrant names, greeting message if provided, cover image if provided).
  • All registry items with product images, names, prices, and variant details.
  • Fulfillment status for each item: how many have been purchased vs. how many are desired. Items that are fully purchased are visually marked and cannot be added to the gift cart.
  • Priority labels (must-have, nice-to-have, dream item) to guide Giver purchasing decisions.
  • Out-of-stock items shown as unavailable with a "Notify When Back" option (leverages existing Back in Stock infrastructure). The Creator also receives a back-in-stock alert when the item is restocked.

5.2.3 Selecting Items for Purchase

The Giver selects one or more available items from the registry. For multi-quantity items (e.g., "4 dinner plates desired, 2 already purchased"), the Giver can choose how many of the remaining quantity to purchase.

Selected items are collected into a gift cart — a temporary, registry-scoped selection that is separate from the merchant's standard Shopify cart. This prevents the mixed-cart complexity of registry and non-registry items in the same checkout.

5.2.4 Checkout (Draft Order)

When the Giver proceeds to checkout:

Step 1 — Shipping destination. If the Creator has enabled "Allow Giver self-shipping," the Giver sees a toggle:

  • "Ship to [Creator's Name]" (default) — gifts ship to the Creator's masked address.
  • "Ship to my address" — Giver provides their own shipping address (for hand-delivery).

If "Allow Giver self-shipping" is disabled by the Creator, this step is skipped entirely. All gifts ship to the Creator.

Step 2 — Draft Order creation. Swym creates a Shopify Draft Order via the Admin API:

  • Line items: the selected registry items and quantities.
  • Shipping address: the Creator's stored address (when shipping to Creator) or left blank for Giver entry (when shipping to self).
  • Order metadata: registry ID, Creator ID, Giver email, gift flag.
  • The Giver is redirected to the Draft Order's checkout URL.

Step 3 — Giver provides email and payment. On the Shopify checkout page, the Giver provides:

  • Email address (required — this is the identity capture mechanism).
  • Payment information.
  • Shipping address (only if they chose "Ship to my address" in Step 1).

The shipping address field is not shown when shipping to the Creator (the address is pre-filled server-side on the Draft Order).

Step 4 — Order placed. The order is submitted. Shopify processes payment. The order webhook fires and Swym's purchase-marking pipeline updates the registry item's fulfillment status.

5.2.5 Post-Purchase

After purchase, the Giver receives:

  • A standard Shopify order confirmation email.
  • A Swym celebration email confirming the gift and showing the registry's updated progress (e.g., "You just helped complete 8 of 12 items on Sarah's registry!").

The Creator receives a purchase confirmation notification (details in Section 7).

5.3 Sales Associate Journey (POS Clienteling)

Store associates can look up a customer's registry via Shopify POS. This is a read-only integration in MVP.

Lookup flow: Associate searches for a customer by name or email in the POS system. If the customer has an active registry with the merchant, it appears in their customer profile. The associate can view:

  • Registry event details (occasion, event date).
  • Registry items and fulfillment status.
  • Registry progress.

Use cases:

  • A customer walks into a store and mentions they have a registry. The associate can pull it up and provide informed assistance.
  • A Giver walks into a store and wants to buy a registry item in person. The associate can look up the registry and ensure they purchase an item that hasn't been fulfilled yet.
  • The associate can suggest additional items based on the registry's theme and what's already been added.

Limitations in MVP: POS integration is read-only. Associates cannot modify the registry, add items, or process registry purchases through POS (purchase-marking for in-store POS transactions is future scope). In-store purchases for registry items must be coordinated manually — the associate can guide the Giver to the online registry checkout, or the Creator can manually mark items as purchased.

5.4 Merchant Journey

5.4.1 Enabling Gift Registry

The merchant enables Gift Registry in the Wishlist Plus admin dashboard. The enablement flow is designed to take under 5 minutes:

Step 1 — Toggle. Merchant toggles Gift Registry on from the Solutions section of the admin.

Step 2 — Theme selection. Merchant selects which Shopify theme to enable the registry on (production or development theme). Merchants will typically enable on a development theme first for review.

Step 3 — Occasion type configuration. Merchant selects which occasion types to support (wedding, baby shower, housewarming, birthday, graduation, holiday, other). Default: all enabled. Merchants can disable occasion types that are not relevant to their category (e.g., a children's toy store might disable "wedding").

Step 4 — Go live. Merchant previews the registry entry point on their storefront and confirms.

Optional deeper configuration (accessible post-enablement):

  • Registry expiry settings (default: event date + 90 days).
  • Branding: registry page header, colors, and layout customization using the same mechanisms available for other Wishlist Plus pages.
  • Notification preferences: which Creator notifications are enabled by default.
  • Default privacy settings: default values for address masking and purchase anonymization (Creators can override).

5.4.2 Ongoing Management

The merchant admin includes:

  • Registry overview dashboard (see Section 9).
  • Individual registry lookup (by Creator name/email).
  • Configuration management.
  • Integration settings for marketing automation triggers.

6. Storefront Experience

6.1 Public-Facing Registry Page (Giver View)

The registry has a dedicated, standalone URL (e.g., store.com/pages/swym-registry/{registry_id} or a customizable slug). This page is publicly accessible via the shared link. No authentication required.

Page structure:

  • Header section: Event details (occasion name, registrant names, event date, countdown to event). Cover image if provided. Greeting message if provided.
  • Registry progress: Visual indicator of overall completion (e.g., "7 of 12 items purchased").
  • Item grid/list: All registry items with product images, names, prices, variant details, desired quantity, quantity already purchased, and priority label. Fully purchased items are visually marked (greyed out or crossed off) and cannot be selected for purchase.
  • Out-of-stock items: Displayed with an "Unavailable" badge and a "Notify When Back" button. Giver can subscribe to a back-in-stock alert for that item. Creator also receives a back-in-stock notification when the item is restocked.
  • Gift cart: A sidebar or bottom bar showing the Giver's selected items and quantities, with a "Proceed to Checkout" CTA.

Key behaviors:

  • The page fetches current product data (price, inventory, images) on every load. It is not a static snapshot.
  • If a product's price changes between when the Creator added it and when the Giver views it, the current price is displayed.
  • The page is responsive and optimized for mobile (most registry sharing happens via text message and social media).

6.2 Creator Management View (within My Lists)

The Creator manages their registry within the existing My Lists page in Wishlist Plus. The registry appears as a distinct section alongside the Creator's wishlists, with clear visual differentiation.

Management capabilities:

  • View all items with fulfillment status, quantities, and priority labels.
  • Add, remove, reorder, and edit items.
  • Edit event details and privacy settings.
  • View registry progress and activity summary.
  • Copy/share the registry link.
  • Archive or complete the registry.

The management view is private — only the Creator (logged-in) can access it.

6.3 "Add to Registry" Button on PDP

When a merchant has Gift Registry enabled and a shopper has an active registry, the PDP must provide a mechanism to add items to the registry in addition to the existing "Add to Wishlist" functionality.

6.3.1 Interaction Rules

The following rules govern the button behavior. Visual design is a UX deliverable; this spec defines the logic.

State: No active registry.

  • The PDP shows the standard "Add to Wishlist" button only (heart icon or text button, per current behavior).
  • No registry-related UI is visible.
  • If the merchant has Gift Registry enabled, a contextual prompt may appear elsewhere on the storefront (e.g., "Create a Gift Registry" banner on the My Lists page) to drive awareness, but the PDP is not the place for this.

State: Active registry exists, Wishlist-only merchant.

  • Not applicable — if the merchant only has Wishlist enabled, registry UI does not appear.

State: Active registry exists, Wishlist + Gift Registry merchant.

  • The PDP shows a smart button that defaults to "Add to Wishlist" (preserving current behavior) with a secondary option to "Add to Registry."
  • Implementation options (UX to decide): split button, dropdown on long-press, secondary text link below the primary button, or a contextual option surfaced after the primary add action.
  • The default action (single click/tap) remains "Add to Wishlist" to avoid disrupting existing behavior.
  • The secondary action ("Add to Registry") must be discoverable but not intrusive.

State: Item already on the registry.

  • The button indicates the item is already on the registry (e.g., checkmark, "On Your Registry" label).
  • Clicking the button could either navigate to the registry management view or allow quantity adjustment.

State: Item out of stock.

  • "Add to Registry" is still available for out-of-stock items (the Creator may want to add it now and hope it restocks before the event).
  • "Add to Wishlist" behavior for OOS items follows existing Back in Stock conventions.

6.3.2 Quantity Selection

In MVP, the "Add to Registry" action adds 1 unit of the selected variant. The Creator can adjust the desired quantity from their registry management view. Future scope: allow quantity selection from the PDP before adding to registry.

6.3.3 Variant Selection

The Creator must select a variant (size, color, etc.) before adding to the registry, same as adding to cart. If no variant is selected, the button prompts variant selection.

7.4 Marketing Automation Integration

All registry events (Section 8.2) flow through the standard Swym marketing automation[r] pipeline. Merchants can configure:

  • Which triggers are enabled (e.g., enable milestone reminders but disable view alerts).
  • Integration with Klaviyo[s]: registry events are sent as Klaviyo events that the merchant can use in their own flows.
  • Integration with Shopify Flow: registry events can trigger Shopify Flow automations.

This enables merchants to build custom automation on top of registry events (e.g., send a targeted campaign to all Givers who viewed but didn't purchase, offer a discount code to Creators whose registry is less than 50% complete 3 days before the event).

8. Technical Approach

8.1 Data Model

Gift Registry uses the registry list type within the Swym List API. The full schema is defined in the List API Specification[t]. Key fields summarized here:

List-level typed metadata (GiftRegistryMeta):

  • occasion_type: Enum (wedding, baby_shower, housewarming, birthday, graduation, holiday, other).
  • occasion_name: Custom event label.
  • event_date: Date of the event.
  • registrant_names: Array of registrant names.
  • shipping_address_id: Reference to stored Creator shipping address.
  • anonymize_address: Boolean (default: true).
  • anonymize_purchases: Boolean (default: true).
  • allow_giver_self_shipping: Boolean (default: false). When true, Givers can choose to ship to their own address.
  • greeting_message: Optional message displayed to Givers.
  • cover_image_url: Optional hero image.

Item-level typed metadata (GiftRegistryItemMeta):

  • priority_label: Enum (must_have, nice_to_have, dream_item).
  • gift_wrap_preferred: Boolean.

Core list fields used:

  • owner_id: The Creator.
  • creator_id: Same as owner_id (Creators create their own registries).
  • access_mode: Default public.
  • lifecycle_state: active → shared → completed or archived.
  • expires_at: Default event_date + 90 days.
  • quantity (item-level): Desired quantity.
  • quantity_fulfilled (item-level): Purchased quantity.
  • purchase_state (item-level): unpurchased | partially_purchased | purchased.
  • purchased_by (item-level): Array of { giver_id, quantity, purchased_at } — hidden from Creator when anonymize_purchases is true.

8.2 Event Schema

Gift Registry emits the following events through the standard Swym event pipeline:

Universal platform events (inherited):

  • list.created — Registry created.
  • list.updated — Registry details modified.
  • list.shared — Share link generated or shared via email/SMS.
  • list.archived — Registry archived.
  • list.item.added — Item added to registry.
  • list.item.updated — Item modified (quantity, priority, notes).
  • list.item.removed — Item removed from registry.
  • list.item.purchased — Item purchase-marked via order webhook.

Registry-specific events:

  • registry.guest.viewed — Giver views the registry page. Payload: { guest_id (nullable), referral_source, city (geo-IP) }.
  • registry.guest.purchased — Giver purchases a registry item. Payload: { item_id, giver_email, quantity_purchased, anonymized, order_id }.
  • registry.completed — All items fully purchased or Creator marks as complete. Payload: { completion_type, total_items, total_fulfilled }.
  • registry.event_date.approaching — Event date within threshold. Payload: { event_date, days_until }.
  • registry.expiring — Registry approaching expiry. Payload: { expires_at, days_until }.
  • registry.item.out_of_stock — Registry item goes out of stock. Payload: { item_id, product_id }.
  • registry.item.back_in_stock — Registry item restocked. Payload: { item_id, product_id }.
  • registry.item.price_dropped — Registry item price decreased. Payload: { item_id, product_id, old_price, new_price }.

8.3 Checkout: Draft Order Flow

The Giver checkout uses Shopify's Draft Order API. This is the most technically complex component of the Gift Registry feature.[u]

8.3.1 Flow

  1. Giver selects items on the registry page and clicks "Proceed to Checkout."
  2. If allow_giver_self_shipping is enabled, Giver selects shipping destination ("Ship to [Creator]" or "Ship to my address").
  3. Swym calls POST /admin/api/2024-01/draft_orders.json on the merchant's Shopify store:
  • line_items: Selected registry items with quantities and variant IDs.
  • shipping_address: Creator's stored address (when shipping to Creator). Omitted when Giver ships to self.
  • note: "Gift Registry order — Registry: [registry_name], Giver: [giver_email]"
  • note_attributes: { registry_id, creator_id, giver_email, gift: true }
  • tags: swym-gift-registry, registry-[registry_id][v]
  1. Swym retrieves the invoice_url from the Draft Order response.
  2. Giver is redirected to the invoice_url — the Shopify-hosted checkout page.
  3. Giver enters email and payment. Shipping address is pre-filled (or Giver enters their own).
  4. Giver completes payment. Shopify processes the order.
  5. Shopify fires the orders/create webhook.
  6. Swym's purchase-marking pipeline matches the order to the registry via note_attributes.registry_id and updates quantity_fulfilled and purchase_state on the relevant items.

8.3.2 Race Condition: Simultaneous Giver Purchases

Two Givers may attempt to purchase the last unit of a registry item simultaneously. Mitigation:

  • Optimistic locking at selection time: When a Giver selects an item for purchase, Swym checks the current quantity_fulfilled against quantity. If the item is already fully purchased, the selection is rejected with a message ("This item was just purchased by someone else!").
  • Final check at Draft Order creation: Before creating the Draft Order, Swym re-checks fulfillment status. If the item was purchased between selection and checkout, the Draft Order is not created and the Giver is shown an updated registry view.
  • Post-order reconciliation: If both Draft Orders are created and both Givers complete checkout (edge case), the second purchase results in over-fulfillment. Swym flags this for the merchant (via admin notification) and the Creator (via email). The merchant handles the return/refund per their standard process. This is an acceptable edge case for MVP — the combination of optimistic locking and pre-checkout check makes it rare.

8.3.3 Draft Order Cleanup

Draft Orders that are created but not completed (Giver abandons checkout) must be cleaned up:

  • Swym polls for incomplete Draft Orders associated with registries.
  • Draft Orders older than 24 hours without a completed payment are voided via the API.[w]
  • Items associated with voided Draft Orders are released back to "available" status on the registry.

8.3.4 Why Draft Orders (Not Checkout Extensions)

Draft Orders are the recommended MVP approach because:

  • Full control over the checkout experience — no shipping address form when shipping to Creator.
  • Native Shopify checkout infrastructure — payment processing, tax calculation, shipping rates all handled by Shopify.
  • No Checkout Extension approval process or maintenance overhead.
  • Order metadata (registry ID, Giver identity) attached natively via note_attributes and tags.
  • Works with all Shopify plans (Checkout Extensions require Shopify Plus for some features).

Future state: If mixed-cart checkout (registry + non-registry items) is needed, Checkout Extensions become the appropriate mechanism. The architecture should not preclude a future migration.[x]

8.4 Address Masking Implementation

The Creator's shipping address is stored by Swym, encrypted at rest, and never exposed to the Giver through any API response or client-side rendering.

  • The registry page (Giver view) shows "Ships to [Creator's First Name]" — no address details.
  • The Draft Order is created server-side with the Creator's full address. The Giver's browser never receives this data.
  • On the Shopify checkout page, the shipping address is pre-filled and non-editable (when shipping to Creator). The Giver sees the city/state but not the street address. (Note: Shopify's checkout may display some address fields; the level of masking achievable within Shopify's checkout UI needs technical validation during implementation.)
  • The Shopify order confirmation email sent to the Giver will include the shipping address (standard Shopify behavior). Open question: whether this can be suppressed or masked for registry orders. If not, the address masking applies to the pre-purchase experience only, and the Creator should be informed during setup that the Giver will see the address on the order confirmation. This must be validated with Shopify's order notification system during technical design.[y]

8.5 Purchase-Marking

Primary mechanism: Shopify orders/create webhook. Swym matches the order to the registry via note_attributes.registry_id and the product/variant IDs in the order line items.

Fallback mechanisms:

  • Manual mark-as-purchased: Creator can manually mark an item as purchased from their management view (for gifts received outside the registry checkout flow, e.g., in-person gifts).
  • Periodic order reconciliation: Swym periodically scans recent orders tagged with swym-gift-registry and reconciles against registry fulfillment status. This catches any orders where the webhook was missed.[z]

Purchase-marking reliability is critical for Gift Registry — a duplicate gift is a real UX failure. The combination of webhook + reconciliation + manual override provides three layers of protection.

8.6 Out-of-Stock Handling (Back in Stock Integration)

Gift Registry integrates with the existing Back in Stock (BiS) infrastructure for out-of-stock items:

  • Giver view: OOS items are displayed with an "Unavailable" badge. The Giver can click "Notify When Back" to subscribe to a back-in-stock alert for that item via the standard BiS flow.
  • Creator notification: When a registry item goes out of stock, the Creator receives an registry.item.out_of_stock notification. When it's restocked, the Creator receives registry.item.back_in_stock.
  • No removal: OOS items are not automatically removed from the registry. The Creator may choose to keep them (hoping for restock before the event) or remove them manually.[aa]

8.7 POS Integration (Read-Only)

Gift Registry data is surfaced in Shopify POS via the existing Swym for Sales infrastructure:

  • Associates can search for a customer by name or email.
  • If the customer has an active registry, it appears in their Swym customer profile (accessible via the POS app extension or SFS-Online web portal).
  • The associate sees: event details, registry items, fulfillment status, and registry progress.
  • This is read-only in MVP. Associates cannot modify the registry or process registry purchases through POS.[ab][ac]

Technical dependency: This requires the unified customer list view described in the Platform spec (Section: Integration Architecture — Swym for Sales). The registry data must be included in the customer profile alongside Wishlist and other list data.

9. Reporting

9.1 Registry Overview Dashboard (Merchant-Facing)

The primary merchant reporting view. Shows aggregate registry performance:

  • Total registries: Created, active, shared, completed, archived.
  • Registry-attributed revenue: Total revenue from orders placed through registry checkout. Contributes to SAV.
  • Giver identities captured: Total unique Giver emails collected through registry purchases. This is the "zero-cost acquisition" metric.
  • Viral coefficient: Average number of unique Giver visitors per registry. Target: 5–10.
  • Average items per registry: Registry "depth" and health indicator.
  • Completion rate: Percentage of registry items that are ultimately purchased. Validates the "active intent" thesis.
  • Incremental AOV: Average order value of registry orders vs. site-wide average.

9.2 Individual Registry Detail View (Merchant-Facing)

Per-registry drill-down accessible from the overview dashboard or via Creator lookup:

  • Registry details (Creator name, occasion, event date, status).
  • Item-level fulfillment status (purchased, partially purchased, unpurchased).
  • Giver activity: number of unique visitors, number of purchases, conversion rate.
  • Timeline of registry events (created, first share, first purchase, completion).

9.3 Giver Acquisition Report (Merchant-Facing)

Dedicated report validating the acquisition engine:

  • Total new Giver emails captured (emails not previously in the merchant's customer list).
  • Giver-to-repeat-buyer conversion: of Givers who purchased via a registry, how many returned to make a non-registry purchase within 30/60/90 days?
  • Revenue from Giver repeat purchases (attributed to the registry as the acquisition source).

This report is critical for proving the business case — it demonstrates that registries don't just generate one-time gift revenue but create an ongoing customer relationship.

9.4 Funnel Metrics (Merchant-Facing)

The full Creator-to-conversion funnel:

Creator Funnel:

  • Registries created → Registries with items added → Registries shared (link copied) → Registries with at least one Giver visit → Registries with at least one purchase → Registries completed.
  • Drop-off rates at each stage.
  • Average time from creation to first share.
  • Average items per registry at time of first share.

Giver Funnel:

  • Registry page visits → Item selections (add to gift cart) → Checkout initiated → Checkout completed.
  • Drop-off rates at each stage.
  • Average time from first visit to first purchase.
  • Average order value per Giver.

9.5 Omni-Channel Metrics (Post-MVP)

For merchants with physical storefronts, measured once POS integration matures:

  • Associate engagement: % of staff actively using registry lookup in POS.

  • POS-attributed purchases: registry-related sales facilitated at the physical register.

9.6 Internal Reports

  • Adoption tracker: Merchant-level feature enablement and active usage (registries created per merchant per month).

  • Cross-merchant benchmark: Comparative performance across merchants (by vertical, plan tier, merchant size) once sufficient data is available.

9.7 SAV Attribution

All purchases made through registry checkout contribute directly to Swym Attributed Value (SAV). Attribution window: 30 days from registry share, configurable per merchant. Registry purchases are tagged with swym-gift-registry for clear attribution.

11. Go-to-Market & Adoption

11.1 Launch Strategy

Gift Registry follows a hybrid launch approach:

Pilot phase (Weeks 1–4): 5–10 hand-picked merchants from the 815 identified in high-affinity categories. Selection criteria:

  • Active Wishlist Plus merchant on Enterprise or Premium plan.
  • Category: Home & Garden, Wedding, Toys, Gifts & Special Events, or Books.
  • Demonstrated interest in gifting features (previous support tickets, feature requests, or CS conversations).
  • Willingness to provide feedback and participate in case study.

Swym co-builds the initial deployment with each pilot merchant, providing hands-on support for enablement, configuration, and early optimization.[ah]

Broader rollout (Weeks 5–8): Based on pilot learnings, roll out to all eligible Enterprise + Premium merchants in target categories via:

  • In-app discovery within Wishlist Plus admin (contextual recommendation: "Your store is in [category] — Gift Registry could help you capture [X] new customers per month").
  • Direct outreach from CS team to high-potential merchants.[ai]
  • Content marketing: case studies from pilot merchants, blog posts, and guides.

General availability (Post Week 8): Evaluate expansion to all plan tiers based on pilot and rollout data.

11.2 Pricing

Gift Registry is included in existing Wishlist Plus Enterprise and Premium plans. No additional per-registry or per-transaction fees in Phase 1. Pricing will be revisited based on usage data and value demonstration from the pilot.[aj]

12. Performance

12.1 Registry Page Load

The public-facing registry page must load within standard ecommerce benchmarks (< 3 seconds on mobile). The page fetches live product data (prices, inventory, images), so performance optimization is critical. Product data should be cached with a short TTL (5 minutes) to balance freshness with load performance.

12.2 Draft Order Creation Latency

The time between "Giver clicks Proceed to Checkout" and "Giver lands on Shopify checkout page" must be under 3 seconds. This includes the Shopify Draft Order API call and redirect. The Giver should see a loading indicator during this transition.

12.3 Purchase-Marking Latency

From order completion to registry fulfillment update: under 60 seconds. This is the time from Shopify's orders/create webhook to the registry item's quantity_fulfilled being updated. Real-time visibility for the Creator and other Givers depends on this latency.

12.4 Concurrent Giver Access

Popular registries (e.g., a celebrity or influencer wedding registry) may receive hundreds of simultaneous visitors. The registry page must handle this without degradation. Caching at the CDN layer (for product data and static registry details) mitigates most load.

12.5 Monitoring

Performance monitoring covers:

  • Registry page load time (p50, p95, p99).
  • Draft Order creation success rate and latency.
  • Purchase-marking latency (webhook receipt to fulfillment update).
  • Notification delivery success rates.
  • Draft Order cleanup (abandoned draft orders voided within 24 hours).

13. Security & Privacy

13.1 Address Privacy

The Creator's shipping address is the most sensitive data in the Gift Registry feature.

  • Stored encrypted at rest in Swym's infrastructure.
  • Never transmitted to the Giver's browser or any client-side code.
  • Injected server-side into the Shopify Draft Order via the Admin API.
  • Access restricted to the Creator (via their authenticated session) and the Swym backend (for order creation).
  • Not included in any API response to Giver-facing endpoints.

Open item: Shopify's standard order confirmation email includes the shipping address. Whether this can be suppressed or masked for registry orders needs validation during technical design. If it cannot be suppressed, the Creator must be clearly informed during setup that the Giver will see the address on the order confirmation email, and the address masking applies to the pre-purchase experience.

13.2 Purchase Anonymization

When anonymize_purchases is enabled:

  • The purchased_by data is stored server-side but excluded from all API responses to the Creator.
  • The Creator sees only quantity_fulfilled updates — not who purchased.
  • The Creator can choose to reveal purchase details at any time (e.g., after the event, to send thank-you notes).
  • Giver identity is visible to the merchant admin (for order management and customer acquisition tracking).

13.3 Giver Data

  • Giver email addresses captured during checkout are stored as customer data in both Shopify (via the order) and Swym (via the registry event).
  • Giver data is subject to the merchant's privacy policy and data handling practices.
  • Swym does not independently market to Givers — the merchant controls all Giver communication.

13.4 Access Control

  • Registry creation and management: authenticated Creator only.
  • Registry viewing (public page): anyone with the link (no authentication).
  • Registry admin access: merchant admin users only.
  • POS access: licensed sales associates via Swym for Sales.
  • No cross-merchant data sharing of registry information.

14. Risks & Open Questions

14.1 Top Risks

Risk

Impact

Mitigation

Draft Order address masking is incomplete

Shopify order confirmation email may reveal Creator's address to Giver, undermining the privacy promise.

Validate during technical design. If address cannot be suppressed on confirmation email, inform Creator during setup. Explore Shopify order notification customization options. Worst case: address masking applies to pre-purchase only and Creator is clearly informed.

Duplicate purchases (race condition)

Two Givers buy the last unit of an item simultaneously, resulting in over-fulfillment and a return.

Three-layer protection: optimistic locking at selection, pre-checkout fulfillment check, post-order reconciliation. Edge case frequency expected to be very low. Merchant notified for manual resolution.

Draft Order checkout abandonment

Givers start checkout but don't complete. Abandoned Draft Orders lock registry items in a "pending" state.

24-hour cleanup job voids incomplete Draft Orders and releases items. Giver receives standard cart abandon trigger.

Merchant adoption

Merchants in target categories may not enable the feature at sufficient scale.

Targeted pilot with hand-picked merchants; in-app contextual discovery; category-specific playbooks; CS-driven activation.

Creator adoption

Shoppers may not create registries (awareness problem).

Merchants must promote the feature on their storefront. Swym provides playbook with banner templates, email campaign suggestions, and social media prompts.

Giver checkout friction

The redirect from registry page to Shopify Draft Order checkout may feel disjointed.

Smooth loading state during redirect. Consistent branding. Registry context visible on checkout page (via order notes). Monitor checkout completion rate closely.

POS integration complexity

Read-only POS integration may be more complex than anticipated if unified customer list view is not ready.

POS is a scope reduction lever. Can be deferred to Phase 2 if it threatens the Q1 timeline.
